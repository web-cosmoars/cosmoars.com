PLANET MODEL OF PLATE TECTONICS

Based on Einstein's Theory of Relativity

O que tem no livro

O livro contém uma abordagem objetiva da tectônica de placas baseada na Teoria da Relatividade de Einstein. Este livro inicia-se com a evolução histórica dos conceitos de espaço, tempo e inércia, os quais terminam incorporados pelas teorias da relatividade especial e geral. Esta é a pedra basilar do modelo de tectônica de placas aqui apresentado e chamado Modelo de Planeta, uma vez que ele envolve a rotação diferencial inercial dos núcleos interno e externo, assim como as outras camadas da Terra.

Sobre o autor, Nivaldo Destro

 

Nivaldo Destro Nascido na cidade de Presidente Prudente, Estado de São Paulo e com cidadania italiana, Nivaldo Destro é Geólogo na Petrobras – Petróleo Brasileiro S.A., onde trabalha como geólogo exploracionista desde 1986. Seu trabalho concentra-se na Geologia Estrutural e Geotectônica, tópicos de suas teses de Mestrado e Doutorado na Universidade Federal de Ouro Preto, Minas Gerais. Ele introduziu novos tipos de falhas e juntas transversais em sistemas distensionais, as chamadas falhas e juntas de alívio. Este livro mostra que a origem genética dessas estruturas transversais pode se aplicar a uma ampla gama de ambientes tectônicos, como um mecanismo universal de deformação.

Lista de capítulos

From a Controversy to a New Theory of Plate Tectonics

Chapter 1 briefly presents the evolution of the ideas on plate tectonics, from its beginning with the Wegener ́s continental drift theory to the current Plume and Plate models. It evaluates the possible reasons for controversies among these models, and proposes a solution based on a new theory of plate tectonics.

  1. Existing Models
  2. Newly Proposed Model
Inertia – Earth's Movement Depends on the Matter of the Rest of the Universe

Chapter 2 reports the concept of inertia, from its proposal by Johannes Kepler in the seventeenth century until its incorporation in the Theory of Relativity. The beautiful and rich scientific evolution of this concept is illustrated with some pictures of the places where great scientists involved in its development lived. It also includes illustrations to clarify some specific and relevant discoveries associated to the consolidation of the concept of inertia.

  1. Introduction
  2. Johannes Kepler
  3. Galileo Galilei
  4. Pierre Gassendi
  5. Renée Descartes
  6. Isaac Newton
  7. Jean Bernard Léon Foucault
  8. Ernst Mach
Space and Time – Earth's Interaction with a Geometric and Flexible Spacetime

Chapter 3 describes the evolution of the concepts of space and time. Initially considered as independent physical entities, they ended up being incorporated in a new single concept, the spacetime in the Theory of Relativity. Here they are inseparable, interdependent and act completely linked to each other.

  1. Introduction
  2. Isaac Newton
  3. Christiaan Huygens
  4. Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz
  5. George Berkeley
  6. Ernst Mach
  7. Prelude to the Theory of Relativity
Theory of Special Relativity – Earth's Uniform Movements

Chapter 4 presents the Theory of Special Relativity that deals with uniform movements, which was published by Albert Einstein in 1905. The Theory of Special Relativity also provided a better understanding of electromagnetism, and had a strong influence in the development of quantum mechanics.

  1. Introduction
  2. Theory of Relativity
  3. Theory of Special Relativity
Theory of General Relativity – Earth's Gravitational Implications

Chapter 5 describes the Theory of General Relativity, published in 1915 by Albert Einstein. Although it incorporates the Theory of Special Relativity, it is distinct from it, since it includes gravity and accelerated movements. Possible spacetime geometries are evaluated according to the theories of special and general relativities.

  1. Introduction
  2. Theory of General Relativity
  3. Spacetime Geometries
Planet Model: From Earth's ́Differentiation to Major Plate Tectonic Features

Chapter 6 presents geological implications of the Theory of Relativity to the Earth's differentiation soon after its formation. It includes calculations of the moments of inertia and angular momentum of the core and other major layers of Earth for the present Earth. The differential rotation among those layers occurs so as to respect the law of conservation of their angular momentum, and explains the perpetuation of Plate Tectonics. The major plate tectonic features, such as mid-ocean ridges, subduction zones, mountain ranges, and linear volcanic chains are evaluated in this newly proposed context.

  1. Introduction
  2. Earth ́s Differentiation as a Result of Changes in the Spacetime Geometry
  3. Planet Model and the Initiation of Plate Tectonics
  4. Major Plate Tectonic Features Based on Planet Model
    1. Divergent Margins
    2. Convergent Margins
    3. Major Features Related to Seafloor Spreading Other than East-West
Linear Volcanic Chains

Chapter 7 deals with the Linear Volcanic Chains, as well as Volcanic Clusters, that are located in the Atlantic Ocean, and which are associated to passive margin basins. It is also herein presented a new interpretation of their origin, according to the Planet Model. It is based on the geometry and the thermal subsidence history of the the oceanic basins. Deflections of the hinge lines formed during the rift stages of these basins play a major role in their development.

  1. Introduction
  2. Origin of Linear Volcanic Chains
  3. Linear Volcanic Chains in the Atlantic Ocean
  4. Cruzeiro do Sul and Vitória-Trindade Volcanic Chains
  5. Walvis, Cameroon and Ascension Volcanic Chains
  6. Linear Volcanic Chains of Northeast Brazil
  7. Guinean Volcanic Chain
  8. New England and the Newfoundland Volcanic chains
  9. Volcanic Clusters in the Atlantic Ocean
